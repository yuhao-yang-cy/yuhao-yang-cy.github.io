## This Site Is Under Construction

Hi! I'm Colin Young, a physics enthusiast working in an international education centre based in Shanghai.

One of my main hobbies on social media is to share various teaching materials that I have created. Thanks to the support of teachers, students, parents, and followers, I've gained some popularity on several platforms. My lecture notes, topical revision sheets, written solutions to past papers and book recommendations have accumulated hundreds of thousands of downloads. I am very happy that these resources are appreciated, and this is the exact reason that I built this site to make these materials available to whoever finds them useful.

For those who are interested, here is more about my personal stories. After completing a bachelor’s degree in physics at Fudan University followed by a master’s degree in theoretical physics at Imperial College, I became crystal clear about the fact that my talents in mathematics and physics are far from that are needed to become a research scienctist, so I decided to pass on my love for physics to the younger generation. And so, my teaching career in international secondary education began in 2011.

Since then, I have been teaching several A-Level syllabuses, including physics, pure mathematics, mechanics, and decision theory, during which I have gained experience and understanding in curriculum design, classroom management and student assessment. I have also helped students with their university applications, among which there are offer holders of Cambridge, Oxford, Imperial, UCL, KCL, Warwick, Manchester, Bristol, Edinburgh, etc. I helped transform students’ personal statements, from which I enjoyed learning about new things and brainstorming ideas. I also developed a series of systematic lectures for Oxford applicants to succeed in ESAT and TMUA tests (previously PAT and MAT tests), together with a set of Oxbridge mock interview questions for different subject options. These have now become part of my job that I find extremely exciting and refreshing.

I am also a firm practitioner of lifelong learning. I have been a keen reader of popular science and other non-fiction literature. I have taught myself LaTeX (for creating lecture slides and hand-outs) and Python programming skills (for implementing scientific simulations and reproducing simple classic games). I am a badminton player and half-marathon runner.
